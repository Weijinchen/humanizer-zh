# Academic Patterns

## Typical AI-like signals in Chinese academic prose

### 1. Definition first, content later, meaning never lands

Common shape:

- starts with a broad definition
- follows with two or three generic points
- ends with a large conclusion unrelated to the evidence

Fix:

- state the concrete problem early
- let the body answer that problem
- close with scope, implication, or limit instead of a slogan

### 2. Translation-like connectors everywhere

High-frequency phrases:

- "对于……来说"
- "基于此"
- "围绕……展开"
- "在这一背景下"
- "值得注意的是"
- "与此同时"

Fix:

- delete connector words when sentence order already shows the relation
- replace with shorter Chinese links
- move clauses into natural Chinese order

### 3. Empty abstraction replacing argument

High-risk words:

- "赋能"
- "重塑"
- "颠覆"
- "深刻影响"
- "开启新篇章"
- "实现高质量发展"

Fix:

- rewrite into actor, action, condition, scope, and outcome
- if no mechanism exists, delete the phrase

### 4. Repetitive three-step template

Signals:

- "首先/其次/最后" appears in many consecutive paragraphs
- each section uses the same numbered cadence
- readers can predict the next paragraph shape before reading it

Fix:

- vary progression by cause, contrast, chronology, example, or limit
- keep numbering only when the content is truly procedural

### 5. Overwritten conclusions

Signals:

- "这标志着……"
- "这意味着一个新时代已经到来"
- "未来已经到来"
- "真正的变革才刚刚开始"

Fix:

- end with the actual implication supported by the text
- name the boundary condition or unresolved issue
- let significance come from the argument, not the slogan

### 6. Literature review that sounds like generated summaries

Signals:

- every paper summary has the same sentence pattern
- contribution statements are flattened
- no distinction between findings, methods, and limitations

Fix:

- vary sentence function
- separate method, result, and limit when summarizing sources
- compare papers on one dimension at a time

### 7. Textbook prose turning into official notice style

Signals:

- too much "全面", "系统", "深入", "持续"
- long ceremonial openings before the concept is explained
- learner gets declarations instead of explanation

Fix:

- define the concept quickly
- explain what it does, when it applies, and why it matters
- keep the teacher voice calm and direct

## Rewrite principle

When in doubt, prefer:

- specific over grand
- argued over declared
- scoped over absolute
- direct over ceremonial
- stable terminology over stylish variation
