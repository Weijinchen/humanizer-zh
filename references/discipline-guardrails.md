# Discipline Guardrails

## Always preserve

- citation placeholders and reference numbering
- formulas, variables, and units
- dataset names, model names, and protocol names
- section logic required by the genre
- domain-specific terms with settled usage

## Do not do these

- do not invent sources
- do not add fake experimental details
- do not convert tentative findings into definitive claims
- do not replace precise technical wording with vague elegance
- do not simplify away methodological limits

## By writing context

### STEM

- keep equations, symbols, and variable names untouched
- preserve cause and condition statements exactly
- avoid replacing technical terms with near-synonyms

### Social science and education

- preserve distinctions among concept, variable, indicator, and interpretation
- keep claims aligned with method and sample scope
- avoid inflating policy or pedagogical significance

### Humanities

- preserve source attribution and quotation boundaries
- keep interpretive nuance and qualification
- avoid flattening voice into generic academic prose

### Medicine and law

- prioritize accuracy over fluency
- do not soften or strengthen normative and risk language
- keep formal terms stable across the whole passage

## Final discipline check

Ask:

1. Did the rewrite change any factual or methodological meaning?
2. Did it alter the force of the claim?
3. Did it disturb a citation, formula, or technical term?
4. Did it improve readability without lowering evidentiary precision?

If any answer is uncertain, revise more conservatively.
